# Moderation Decision Appeals Policy

## Purpose
This policy establishes a formal process for content authors or package owners to appeal decisions made by the Governance Team regarding content takedowns, acceptable use violations, or moderation actions against their prompt packages.

## Eligibility
Only the original author or designated package owner of a prompt package that has been subject to a moderation action can submit an appeal.

## Appeal Process
To submit an appeal, the package owner must:
1. File an appeal within 14 days of the original moderation decision notification.
2. Submit a clear and detailed explanation of why the decision should be overturned.
3. Provide new evidence, technical clarification, or context that was not previously considered during the initial review.

## Review Process
1. **Receipt**: The Governance Team will acknowledge the appeal within 48 hours.
2. **Independent Review**: The appeal will be assigned to a Governance Team member who was not involved in the original moderation decision to ensure impartiality.
3. **Assessment**: The assigned reviewer will assess the new evidence against the Acceptable Use Policy and original findings.
4. **Final Decision**: The reviewer will make a final determination to either:
   - **Uphold** the original moderation decision.
   - **Overturn** the decision and restore the prompt package to the registry.

## Finality
Decisions reached through the appeal process are considered final and binding. Repeatedly appealing the same decision without new evidence is considered a violation of community guidelines.
