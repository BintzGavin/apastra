# Acceptable Use Policy

This Acceptable Use Policy defines the standards for content and conduct within the registry. By publishing, submitting, or consuming content, you agree to abide by these terms. This policy ensures the safety, legality, and integrity of the registry for all users.

## 1. Prohibited Content

The following categories of content are strictly prohibited from being published or stored in the registry:

- **Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM):** Any content depicting or promoting the sexual abuse or exploitation of minors.
- **Malware and Malicious Code:** Prompts designed to generate, distribute, or facilitate malware, ransomware, viruses, or any code intended to disrupt or gain unauthorized access to systems.
- **Hate Speech and Harassment:** Content that promotes violence, incites hatred, discriminates against protected groups, or engages in targeted harassment of individuals or organizations.
- **Personally Identifiable Information (PII) Leaks:** Prompts that intentionally solicit, expose, or facilitate the unauthorized distribution of sensitive personal data (e.g., social security numbers, financial information, non-public contact details).
- **Copyright and Intellectual Property Infringement:** Content that infringes upon the copyrights, trademarks, or intellectual property rights of others. You must have the right to publish the content you submit.
- **Deceptive or Fraudulent Content:** Prompts designed for phishing, scams, generating disinformation, or impersonating individuals or entities for malicious purposes.
- **Illegal Acts:** Any content intended to facilitate or encourage activities that are illegal under applicable laws.

## 2. Conduct and Usage Standards

- **Authenticity:** Users must accurately represent the purpose and capabilities of their submitted prompts and models.
- **Security:** Do not attempt to bypass or undermine the registry's security, moderation, or governance mechanisms.
- **Rate Limiting and Abuse:** Do not engage in activities that impose an unreasonable or disproportionately large load on the registry infrastructure.

## 3. Enforcement Mechanisms

The registry employs a single-custodian moderation model to ensure compliance with this policy. Enforcement mechanisms include:

- **Automated Scans:** All submissions are subject to automated scanning (including schema validation, secrets detection, and heuristic policy checks) prior to and after publication.
- **Community Reports:** Users are encouraged to report content that violates this policy using the established community reporting workflow.
- **Human Moderation:** A dedicated moderation team reviews escalated content, community reports, and high-risk submissions.

## 4. Consequences of Violation

Violations of this Acceptable Use Policy will result in enforcement actions, which may include, but are not limited to:

- **Takedowns:** Immediate removal or unlisting of the violating prompt or package.
- **Warning:** Issuance of a formal warning to the publisher.
- **Trusted Publisher Revocation:** Removal of "Trusted Publisher" status and associated badges.
- **Registry Ban:** Temporary or permanent suspension of the user's or organization's ability to publish to the registry.
- **Legal Action:** In cases of severe violations (e.g., CSAM, illegal acts), we may report the activity to appropriate law enforcement authorities.

For information on appealing a moderation decision, please refer to the `appeals.md` policy.
