# Drift Alerts Policy

## 1. Context and Scope
This policy governs the handling of drift detection alerts generated by canary suites, formalizing escalation rules and auto-rollback conditions within the registry metadata store.

## 2. Alert Thresholds
- **Pass**: Canary suite runs must meet the `pass_rate` threshold defined in the baseline policy.
- **Fail/Alert**: If the suite fails to meet the threshold, a drift alert is immediately generated and logged to the append-only registry metadata store.

## 3. Escalation Paths
- All drift alerts MUST be dispatched to the designated on-call response channel (e.g., `#promptops-alerts`).
- Governance Admins (@apastra/governance-admins) are responsible for acknowledging and triaging the alert within the SLA defined in their respective team charter.

## 4. Auto-Rollback Rules
- If a critical production canary suite fails consecutively across a defined number of trials (e.g., 3 consecutive failures to account for transient provider errors), an auto-rollback is mandated.
- An auto-rollback is executed by appending a new promotion record to the registry metadata store that points the production channel back to the last known-good package digest (baseline).
- The rollback promotion record must explicitly reference the drift alert ID as its justification.

## 5. Auditability
- All generated drift alerts, escalation acknowledgments, and subsequent auto-rollbacks (via promotion records) are strictly append-only and fully auditable.
